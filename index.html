1. Stack ADT

1. Imagine a pile of books, with books stacked one over the other. From this pile of books, you can either put another book on top or remove a book from the top.
2. The book which is at the bottom of the pile is the last one to be taken out, while the books at the top are removed first. Books can only be added to the top of the pile.
3. Let the action of putting a book on the top be called as push and let the action of removing a book be called pop. A type of structure, similar to the example of the pile of books, can be represented as a data structure. Such a data structure is known as a stack.

Objectives of this experiments are :
1. Understand the concept of stacks
2. Understand the basic operations of stacks
3. Practice the operations of stacksz

2. Queue ADT

1. Imagine a line of people waiting at a ticket counter. People are served in the order they come, that is, people who come first are served first whereas people who come later are served after that.
2. Let the action of someone joining a queue be called enqueue and someone being serve and getting out of the queue be called dequeue.
3. A type of structure, similar to the example of the line of people, can be represented as a data structure. Such a data structure is known as a queue.

1. Understand the concept of Queues
2. Understand the basic operations of Queues
3. Practice the operations of Queues

3. Linked List ADT

1. Linked List can be defined as collection of objects called nodes that are randomly stored in the memory.
2. A node contains two fields i.e. data stored at that particular address and the pointer which contains the address of the next node in the memory.
3. The last node of the list contains pointer to the null.

Objectives of this experiments are :
1. Understand the concept of Linked list
2. Understand the basic operations of Linked list

4. Binary Search Tree

Binary Search Tree is a node-based binary tree data structure which has the following properties:
1. The left subtree of a node contains only nodes with keys lesser than the node’s key.
2. The right subtree of a node contains only nodes with keys greater than the node’s key.
3. The left and right subtree each must also be a binary search tree.

Objectives of this experiments are :
1. Understand the concept of Binary Search Tree
2. Understand the basic operations of Binary Search Tree
3. Practice the operations of Binary Search Tree

Prerequisite :

5. Graph

A Graph is a non-linear data structure consisting of vertices and edges. The vertices are sometimes also referred to as nodes and the edges are lines or arcs that connect any two nodes in the graph. More formally a Graph is composed of a set of vertices( V ) and a set of edges( E ). The graph is denoted by G(E, V).

Objectives of this experiments are :
1. Understand the concept of Graph
2. Understand the basic operations of Graph
3. Practice the operations of Graph
